# GridPulse Brand System Spec
_Last updated: 2026-04-09_

## 1) Executive recommendation

GridPulse should be repositioned from **“energy demand forecasting dashboard”** to an **energy intelligence operating layer** for utilities, IPPs, traders, and grid-adjacent analytics teams.

The current product already has the right raw ingredients for that move:
- a dark, control-room style UI
- role-based personas
- multi-tab decision workflows
- regional grid views
- forecasting, backtesting, alerts, and simulation concepts
- presentation/meeting mode
- an energy news ribbon
- a technical, credible ML/data foundation

That means the redesign should **not** feel like a consumer SaaS re-skin. It should feel like a product maturing from “portfolio demo / analytics dashboard” into **category-grade energy operations software**.

---

## 2) What the brand should stand for

### Core idea
**GridPulse helps energy teams see demand earlier, assess risk faster, and act with more confidence.**

### Brand promise
**Operational clarity for volatile energy systems.**

### Brand attributes
Use these as the governing personality filters:

- **Credible** — built for serious operational users, not hype
- **Calm** — composed under pressure; never frantic
- **Intelligent** — analytical without sounding academic
- **Precise** — language is measured, specific, and confident
- **Modern infrastructure** — feels like software for the next grid, not legacy utility software
- **Human-in-the-loop** — assists judgment; does not pretend to replace operators

### What it should not feel like
- generic AI startup
- crypto / cyberpunk gimmick
- climate nonprofit branding
- flashy fintech
- “cute dashboard”
- over-ornamented enterprise BI

## 3) Brand strategy and market position

## Category position
Do **not** position GridPulse as “just another forecasting dashboard.”

Position it as:

> **An energy intelligence platform for demand visibility, forecast confidence, and grid-aware operational decision support.**

### Stronger market framing
GridPulse sits in the overlap of:
- grid analytics
- demand forecasting
- operational intelligence
- weather-informed power planning
- energy market awareness
- scenario-based decision support

### Recommended market position
**For utility, IPP, ISO/RTO-adjacent, and energy trading teams that need faster operational visibility, GridPulse turns fragmented load, weather, forecast, and model-performance signals into one decision-ready system.**

### Positioning statement
**GridPulse is an energy intelligence operating platform that unifies demand forecasting, grid visibility, model confidence, and scenario planning so energy teams can interpret changing conditions faster and act with greater confidence.**

### Why this position works
It expands the product beyond “forecasting” without becoming vague. It also creates room for:
- alerts
- generation mix
- scenario simulation
- market overlays
- model governance
- enterprise APIs
- future suite expansion

## 4) Naming strategy

## Keep or rename?
**Recommendation: keep “GridPulse” as the master brand** unless you want to broaden beyond energy later.

Why keeping it works:
- strong metaphor: pulse = heartbeat, monitoring, live system state
- sector-relevant: grid is explicit and useful
- memorable and clean
- can scale into a product suite easily

### When to rename
Rename only if one of these becomes true:
1. you want the platform to expand beyond power/energy into broader infrastructure intelligence
2. trademark or domain problems emerge
3. you want a more premium, less literal enterprise name

## Recommended naming architecture

### Best option: endorsed suite architecture
Use **GridPulse** as the master brand, then attach product descriptors.

Examples:
- **GridPulse Forecast**
- **GridPulse Watch**
- **GridPulse Dispatch**
- **GridPulse Market**
- **GridPulse Risk**
- **GridPulse Studio**
- **GridPulse API**

This is the cleanest, strongest enterprise architecture.

### If you want a sharper “platform” parent brand later
You could evolve to:
- **GridPulse OS**
- **GridPulse Platform**
- **GridPulse Command**
- **GridPulse Intelligence**

### If you decide to rename entirely
Only a few directions feel stronger than GridPulse:

#### Best rename candidates
- **PeakSignal**
- **LoadLens**
- **FluxGrid**
- **LatticeGrid**
- **DispatchIQ**
- **PowerSignal**
- **GridIntel**
- **ReserveOS**

### Recommendation if renaming
If you rename, my strongest options are:
1. **PeakSignal** — best for commercial polish and enterprise clarity
2. **LoadLens** — smart, analytical, narrower and cleaner
3. **FluxGrid** — more modern/tech-forward
4. **DispatchIQ** — strong if the product moves closer to operational actioning

Still, **GridPulse remains the best overall choice** for the current product.

## 10) Color system

The current dark navy + red system is directionally right, but the red accent reads more “alert” than “intelligence.” Shift the accent structure toward **electric cyan / aqua / energy lime** for core action and reserve red for risk states.

## Recommended core palette

### Neutral / surface palette
- **Obsidian** — `#0B1020`
- **Midnight Grid** — `#11182D`
- **Control Surface** — `#17223B`
- **Panel Border** — `#263556`
- **Graphite Text** — `#A8B3C7`
- **Cloud Text** — `#DDE6F2`
- **Pure Signal** — `#F7FAFC`

### Primary accent palette
- **Pulse Cyan** — `#38D0FF`
- **Grid Blue** — `#4A7BFF`
- **Aurora Teal** — `#2DE2C4`

### Secondary accent palette
- **Reserve Lime** — `#B8F36B`
- **Solar Amber** — `#FFC857`
- **Event Coral** — `#FF6B6B`

### Semantic palette
- **Success** — `#2BD67B`
- **Warning** — `#FFB84D`
- **Critical** — `#FF5C7A`
- **Info** — `#68B6FF`

## How to use color
- **Cyan / blue** = primary interaction, selection, active tab, important data emphasis
- **Teal** = model confidence / forecast intelligence
- **Lime** = positive reserve / healthy operating status / renewable signal
- **Amber** = caution / watch state
- **Coral-red** = risk, severe deviations, outages, critical alerts

## Avoid
- too much saturated red in the default experience
- bright green + bright red combinations for core charts
- overly neon cyberpunk gradients

## Gradient usage
Use gradients sparingly and structurally:
- header bands
- hero section backgrounds
- KPI glow edges
- selected states
- map / topology visuals

Recommended gradients:
- `linear-gradient(135deg, #11182D 0%, #17223B 55%, #102A43 100%)`
- `linear-gradient(135deg, #38D0FF 0%, #4A7BFF 50%, #2DE2C4 100%)`

## 11) Typography

Use typography that feels precise, modern, and enterprise-safe.

## Recommended system

### Primary UI / brand font
**Inter**
- best default for dashboards, product UI, and marketing body copy
- crisp, highly legible, flexible

### Secondary display font
Choose one:
- **Sora** — modern, slightly futuristic, clean
- **Plus Jakarta Sans** — polished and premium
- **Manrope** — excellent for product-forward brands

### Best pairing
**Inter + Sora**

### Usage
- **Display / hero / product section titles:** Sora
- **UI, tables, controls, body:** Inter
- **Data-heavy labels:** Inter Medium
- **Numeric KPI emphasis:** Inter SemiBold or Bold with tabular numbers

## Typographic scale
### Web
- Hero: 56 / 64
- H1: 40 / 48
- H2: 32 / 40
- H3: 24 / 32
- H4: 20 / 28
- Body L: 18 / 28
- Body: 16 / 24
- UI Small: 14 / 20
- Meta: 12 / 16

### Product
- Page title: 28 / 34
- Section title: 20 / 26
- Card title: 16 / 22
- KPI label: 11 / 14 uppercase or semicap
- KPI value: 28–40 depending on context
- Table copy: 13–14 / 18–20

## 12) Logo system

## Core recommendation
Drop literal lightning-bolt clichés.

Use a logo system built around one of these ideas:

### Concept A — Pulse band
A simplified waveform / pulse line embedded into a grid-aligned rectangular or circular mark.

Meaning:
- monitoring
- system heartbeat
- dynamic load movement

### Concept B — GP monogram
A custom monogram where:
- the **G** is constructed as a partial ring / field line
- the **P** stem becomes a rising signal path or data trace

Meaning:
- enterprise software
- scalable across app icon, favicon, social avatar

### Concept C — Node-field mark
Small connected nodes creating a minimal abstract grid cell with a highlighted “pulse” node.

Meaning:
- intelligence network
- grid systems
- signal propagation

## Best direction
**Concept B + A hybrid**
A clean monogram/icon that implies both **grid structure** and **signal movement**.

## Logo behavior
- primary full lockup: icon + wordmark
- compact icon for favicon/app icon
- monochrome version for docs
- inverse version for dark UI
- high-contrast version for light landing page sections

## Wordmark treatment
Use a custom or semi-custom wordmark:
- emphasize **Grid**
- keep **Pulse** slightly brighter or accented
- subtle distinction between infrastructure and signal

Example styling direction:
- `Grid` in Cloud Text
- `Pulse` in Pulse Cyan

## 13) UI design system direction

## Overall product design language
**Premium operations software**, not generic Bootstrap admin theme.

### Principles
1. **Quiet by default** — let data carry visual weight
2. **Color only where meaning exists**
3. **Information hierarchy before decoration**
4. **Confidence must be visible**
5. **Decision flow beats dashboard sprawl**

## Component style
- cards: softer corners, tighter shadows, subtle border illumination
- tabs: underlined or pill hybrid, clearly active, less default-bootstrap feel
- selectors: compact, crisp, segmented where possible
- charts: high contrast, fewer chart junk elements, better annotation language
- KPI cards: more hierarchy, less “tile grid”
- alerts: graduated severity, not just red boxes
- news ribbon: calmer and more integrated or moved to contextual intelligence rail

## Recommended UI tokens

### Radius
- 6 for controls
- 10 for cards
- 14 for larger panels or hero surfaces

### Border
- 1px default
- 1px elevated with slightly brighter edge on hover/focus

### Shadow
Very restrained:
- panels: low blur, low opacity
- hover: barely perceptible lift
- selected: border + inner glow, not heavy drop shadow

### Motion
- 150–200ms standard
- 240ms for panel reveal
- no bouncy motion
- no decorative parallax inside product

## 15) Core flow redesign

GridPulse should feel less like “open tab, inspect chart” and more like a structured decision loop.

## Recommended primary flow
### Flow 1 — Daily operator loop
1. Land on **Overview**
2. See key system status, forecast direction, risk flags
3. Drill into **Forecast**
4. Inspect confidence, regional changes, weather drivers
5. Move to **Risk**
6. Check alerts / anomalies / severe conditions
7. Use **Scenarios**
8. Compare “what if” conditions
9. Export/share in **Briefing mode** or presentation mode

### Flow 2 — Analyst loop
1. Start in **Forecast** or **Models**
2. Compare model performance and forecast divergence
3. Review feature importance / confidence / error bands
4. Pivot into **Grid** for operational implications
5. Save/share a briefing or snapshot

### Flow 3 — Executive / stakeholder loop
1. Land in **Overview**
2. Read 3–5 high-signal cards
3. Open narrative summary panel
4. Enter presentation mode
5. Review selected regions and risk windows

## Design implication
The landing screen should answer:
- what changed?
- what matters?
- where is risk rising?
- how confident are we?
- what should I inspect next?

- Forecast

## 22) Data visualization rules

Data viz is part of the brand.

## Chart principles
- fewer simultaneous colors
- stronger labeling
- explicit annotation of risk windows and inflection points
- confidence bands must be elegant and readable
- semantic color should map cleanly across product

## Suggested color assignments
- actual demand: Cloud Text or bright neutral
- forecast baseline: Pulse Cyan
- alternate model: Grid Blue
- confidence band: transparent cyan/teal fill
- risk/event marker: Coral or Amber
- renewable share: Reserve Lime or Teal
- alerts: Amber → Coral by severity

## Do not
- use rainbow palettes
- overload charts with too many toggles visible at once
- rely on color alone for critical distinctions
